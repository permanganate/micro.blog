Casters

Sep 16, 2013

Tonight I installed the casters that I picked up at Home Depot yesterday. It’s unfortunate that finding casters that have a) steel/metal wheels, and b) are made in the USA, is so difficult. I know they exist, from some deep googling, but the manufacturer’s websites request that you submit info for a quote, which makes me think they don’t sell directly to consumers in quantities of 4.

To attach the casters, I used 1" lag screws through the batten and a bit into the floor of the chest. Everything sounded ok as I cranked them down, and nothing went through the floor into the chest. Slowly flipped the chest over onto the casters… That should be the last time it’s upside down.

Saw Till

Sep 15, 2013

I worked more on the saw till and sectional wall today. I plowed the kerfs for the saws to rest in. I think it might be easier to plow the ones on this inside first, then the two on the outside–I ended up using little wedges to keep the inner kerfs open while I made the outer ones. While cutting the semi-circle on the top, I ran into the same problem I had while cutting all the dovetails for the carcase, that poplar kind smushes and doesn’t cut a super crisp line.

Since Chris or Rob did all the tongue and groove cuts for the floors, I wanted to try doing that myself for the sectional wall. Getting the wide blade assembly and tongue blade all dialed in on my Veritas plow plane took me a while this first time, but once it was set up it worked fine. Jackie walked in while I was pushing the entire workbench around, and I think she understood my talk about a heavier workbench in the future.

Edit, 2013-09-20: I finished the cleats for the saw till and mounted them to the carcase with clinch rose head nails, and started work on the cleats for the rear corral area of the floor.
